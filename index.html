📈
Progression Within Phase 1
How to move forward week by week
  • Week 1–2: Focus entirely on form and machine settings. Note your weight settings in a notebook or phone. Effort should feel moderate — you should be able to speak in short sentences.
  • Week 3: If the last 2 reps of each set feel easy, add the smallest available weight increment (usually 2.5–5kg on machines).
  • Week 4: Aim for all sets feeling comfortably challenging. By end of week 4 you should be completing all sets without needing to rest mid-set. This is your Phase 1 graduation marker.
  • Cardio: Add 2 minutes to your cross trainer time each week. Target 15 mins by end of week 4.

Why Your Other Activities Are Perfect
🏊
Breaststroke Swimming
Breaststroke is one of the best exercises for your specific situation. It works the inner thighs and hip adductors that support your knees, it's completely non-weight-bearing (zero joint compression), the frog kick gently mobilises the SI joint, and it burns 300–400 calories per hour at medium pace. It also complements the gym by working different muscle angles.
🧘
Lee Mills Body Balance
Body Balance combines yoga, Pilates and tai chi — it's genuinely ideal alongside your gym programme. It lengthens the muscles your gym sessions shorten, trains the deep stabilisers of your spine (crucial for discs and SI joint), develops the balance and proprioception that protects arthritic knees, and reduces inflammation through slow mindful movement.
📅 Flexible? That's Fine. If life moves things around, the core rule is: keep at least one full rest day between Tuesday and Saturday gym sessions — Wednesday does that perfectly. Friday swim before Saturday gym is your tightest gap, so if your legs feel heavy on Saturday, reduce leg press weight slightly. Your Monday class is so gentle for you that it genuinely doesn't count as exercise stress.

Joint Care

How this programme protects and strengthens your knees, discs and SI joint — not just works around them.

The Underlying Philosophy The muscles surrounding a joint are its best protection. Strong quads, hamstrings and glutes take load away from your knee cartilage. Strong back extensors and deep core muscles reduce pressure on your discs. Strong glutes and hip rotators stabilise the SI joint. Every exercise in this programme serves double duty: it builds lean muscle AND protects your joints.
🦵 Arthritic Knees
  • Leg press is far safer than squats or lunges for arthritic knees — you control the range of movement and there's no spinal load
  • Keep feet at hip-width or slightly wider on leg press. Never let knees cave inward
  • Stop leg press range at 90 degrees if you feel discomfort — don't go to full bend
  • Leg curls build hamstrings — the hamstrings act as a second ACL, stabilising the knee from behind
  • Hip abductor work corrects the knock-knee pattern that accelerates arthritis
  • If knees are particularly sore on a given day, reduce leg press weight by 20% and avoid the cross trainer — recumbent bike instead
🔙 Disc Issues
  • Zero exercises in this programme involve spinal compression (no barbell squats, no deadlifts, no overhead press standing)
  • All pressing and pulling is done seated with a supported back
  • Core work uses anti-rotation and anti-extension patterns (Pallof press, dead bug) — safer than crunches for discs
  • If you feel any tingling, numbness or shooting sensation down a leg during any exercise — stop immediately and rest
  • The seated row is one of the best exercises for disc issues — it strengthens the erector spinae and rhomboids that support spinal posture
  • Avoid forward-bending under load at all times
🍑 SI Joint Irritation
  • The SI joint is stabilised primarily by the gluteus maximus and deep hip rotators — which is why hip abductor and glute work is prioritised
  • Avoid asymmetric loading (e.g. single-leg exercises, carrying bags on one side) particularly on flare-up days
  • The Pallof press is ideal — it trains the pelvis to resist rotation without loading the SI joint directly
  • Breaststroke frog kick gently mobilises the SI joint — the warmth of the pool also reduces irritation
  • Body Balance's tai chi-influenced lateral movements help restore balanced SI joint mechanics
  • On SI flare days: skip leg press, do upper body only, and use the recumbent bike at low resistance instead
⚠️ Red Flags — Always Stop If You Notice: Sharp or shooting pain in the leg (possible nerve involvement) · Sudden increase in back pain after a set · Dizziness or chest pain during cardio · Knee swelling after a session (apply ice, rest, review load next session)
💬 Talk to the Gym Staff Torbay Leisure staff can do an induction on any machine you're unsure about. Show them your joint history — good gyms appreciate members who ask. They may also be able to suggest modifications specific to their equipment.
